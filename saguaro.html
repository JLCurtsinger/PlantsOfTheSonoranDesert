Saguaro

Habitat range

Saguaro Cacti are endemic to the Sonoran Desert (meaning they can only be found there and nowhere else in the world) and can be found in Southern Arizona and Sonora, Mexico and Baja California, Mexico, with a few outliers in California from sea level to around 4000 feet in elevation. In the far north of their range, they prefer warmer, south facing slopes. They are easy to identify by their columnar shape with accordion-like ridges that are covered in spikes. They can grow to over 40 feet tall but can take up to 10 years to reach the size of a thumb.
