Desert Willow

Habitat range

The Desert Willow is a tree that can be found in the Chihuahuan, Great Basin, Mojave, and Sonoran deserts. They can grow up to 40 feet tall and produce beautiful purple and white flowers from April-October. Gargling tea made from Desert Willow bark can help with sore throats. They also have astringent and anti-fungal properties.

Plant Identification

Plant Uses

Desert Willows can be identified by their long narrow leaves that can be 4-12 inches long and a half inch wide. They have smooth brown bark and beautiful purple and white flowers during bloom season.
