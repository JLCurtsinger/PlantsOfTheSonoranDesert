Prickly Pear

Habitat range

Prickly Pear cacti have a wide habitat range and can be found throughout the Southwestern United States and Northwestern Mexico most commonly. They can also be found in nearly every other state to some degree and various countries throughout the world as well. They prefer well-draining soil that is sandy/gravelly and prefer slightly acidic.

Plant Identification

Plant Uses

Prickly pear cacti are easy to identify by their segmented, oval shaped pads that form branches and also by the red fruits that can be found on the tops of their pads when in season. The pads of prickly pear cacti can be cooked and eaten for a nutritious meal. The fruits are edible and nutritious as well. Medicinally, prickly pear can be used to treat liver issues, ulcers, and more.
